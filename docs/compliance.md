# Compliance & Positioning

Investment advice in India is regulated by SEBI. FundXRay is deliberately built as an **analytics and transparency tool over public regulatory disclosures**, not an advisory product. This constraint is a design input, not an afterthought.

## Hard rules

**Never:**
- "buy", "sell", "switch", "exit", "best fund", "top pick"
- rankings or star ratings that imply recommendation
- projected or predicted returns for a specific scheme
- personalised suitability judgements
- language implying a fund is good or bad

**Always:**
- describe what *is*, sourced and dated
- show assumptions explicitly and let the user change them
- attach the disclosure date to every holdings-derived number
- carry the standard risk disclaimer
- state clearly that the project is unaffiliated with AMFI, SEBI, Angel One, or any AMC

## Framing that works

| Avoid | Use |
|---|---|
| "This fund is overpriced" | "This fund's active share is 12% and its TER is 1.75%" |
| "You should switch to direct" | "The same scheme's direct plan has a TER of 0.62%; at your contribution and horizon the difference compounds to ₹X under a Y% return assumption" |
| "Reduce your HDFC Bank exposure" | "HDFC Bank is 11.4% of your look-through portfolio as of 30 June 2026" |

The discipline makes the product better. "Here is what you own and what you pay, sourced and dated" is more trustworthy — and more useful — than another opinion engine.

## Data usage

All sources are public regulatory disclosures or accessed under the terms of a personal API account. Review Angel One SmartAPI terms before redistributing any market data; cache and derive rather than republish raw feeds.
